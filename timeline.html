1986 – 2026

The timeline

Five eras, one direction of travel — from a collapsed economy to a nation accelerating.

1986

Foundation

A new government inherits a country of broken institutions and a collapsed economy: GDP per capita of $330, 60 MW of electricity nationwide, and 56% of citizens living in poverty. Reconstruction begins.

1990s

Stabilisation

Currency reform, a new constitution, and the first direct election. In 1997, Universal Primary Education opens the school gates to every child — adult literacy begins its climb from 43% toward 70% and beyond.

2000s

Growth

Steady growth returns. The landmark Refugee Act of 2006 opens the door that 1.8 million people would come through, and the Vision 2040 blueprint is approved. The oldest export — coffee — becomes a modern engine of rural income.

2010s

Transformation

Expressways, new Nile crossings and industrial parks reconnect the country and its region. The 51 km Kampala–Entebbe Expressway opens in 2018 alongside the 525 m Source of the Nile Bridge at Jinja.

2020s

Acceleration

Karuma's 600 MW comes online in 2024, lifting national capacity past 2,000 MW — thirty-four times where this story began. Poverty stands at 16%, GDP per capita at $986, and the paved network at 6,850 km.

2026

The record continues

In the neonatal wards of new regional hospitals, the next forty years have already begun.
