The National Ledger · 1986–2026

Forty years measured in national capacity

GDP per capita[4]
$330→$986

Roughly a threefold rise in real terms, sustained across four decades of rapid population growth.

Electricity generation[6]
60 MW→2,048 MW

From a single ageing dam to Karuma, Isimba and a national grid with surplus for the region.

Poverty[4]
56%→16%

The national poverty headcount fell by more than two-thirds from its 1992 measurement.

Refugees hosted[5]
1.8M

Africa’s largest host nation, under one of the world’s most progressive refugee frameworks.

The Journey

Forty years in motion

Six eras, one direction of travel.

ERA 01
1986

Foundation

The NRM takes power. Reconstruction begins from a collapsed economy and broken institutions.

ERA 02
1990s

Stabilisation

Currency reform, a new constitution, the first direct election — and Universal Primary Education opens the gates.

ERA 03
2000s

Growth

Steady growth returns. A landmark Refugee Act is passed and the Vision 2040 blueprint is approved.

ERA 04
2010s

Transformation

Expressways, new Nile crossings and industrial parks reconnect the country and its region.

ERA 05
2020s

Acceleration

Karuma is commissioned and national capacity passes 2,000 MW as first oil approaches.

ERA 06
Beyond

Next Horizon

Achievements Atlas

Transformation across national life

Plate I

Economy & Livelihoods

Real income per person roughly tripled; coffee and value addition anchor an economy far beyond subsistence.

Plate II

Energy & Infrastructure

From 60 MW to 2,048 MW; 6,850 km of tarmac on a 21,000 km national network.[1]

Plate III

Social Progress

The first tolled expressway, new Nile bridges and national connectivity turned distance into opportunity.[3]

Plate IV

Health, Education & Human Capital

UPE opened the classroom to every child; the HIV turnaround became a global model; literacy rose from 43% to over 70%.

Plate V

Regional Responsibility

1.8 million refugees hosted with land and the right to work — the world calls it the Uganda Model.[5]

Plate VI

Stability & Continuity

Forty years of stability — the precondition for investment, schooling, health and growth.
