01 · Where it began

“This is not a mere change of guard — it is a fundamental change.”

— Spoken at the swearing-in · Kampala · 1986

i

A collapsed economy

Hyperinflation and a broken currency, after years of conflict and misrule.

ii

Darkness after dusk

One ageing dam, just 60 megawatts, and firewood meeting almost all energy.

iii

A cycle of instability

Decades of coups, dictatorship and civil war — a nation desperate for peace.

09 · The next chapter

From a peasant economy to a modern, prosperous nation

The foundation is laid. The decade ahead is industrialisation, oil and Vision 2040 — turning four decades of groundwork into shared prosperity.

First oil

With commercial reserves confirmed at Lake Albert, production is set to begin in 2026 via a pipeline to the coast.

≈ 230,000 barrels/day at peak

An industrial economy

From under 100 factories to thousands — steel, minerals and manufacturing now exporting across the region.

Targeting a tenfold economy

A regional power

A surplus of clean hydropower, a growing trade hub and a stabilising force across East Africa.

Vision 2040

“A transformed Ugandan society — from a peasant to a modern and prosperous country.”

— The Vision 2040 aspiration

Forty years ago, a nation in ruins.
Today, a nation on the rise.

The work continues — but the distance travelled is a story worth telling, and worth honouring.
