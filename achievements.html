Achievements

Five sectors. Forty years. Every figure carries a numbered source you can check on the sources page.

01 · ENERGY

A grid multiplied thirty-four times

Karuma's 600 MW came online in 2024, joining Isimba and Bujagali on the Victoria Nile and lifting national generation capacity past two thousand megawatts.

  • Karuma Hydropower — 600 MW, commissioned 2024
  • Isimba and Bujagali stations on the Victoria Nile
  • Lake Albert oilfields confirmed; production ahead

60 → 2,048 MWinstalled capacity [6]

02 · ROADS

A country reconnected

The tarmac backbone reaches every region. The 51 km Kampala–Entebbe Expressway cut the airport run from over ninety minutes to about forty, and the 525 m Source of the Nile Bridge carries the east.

  • Kampala–Entebbe Expressway, opened 2018 [3]
  • Source of the Nile Bridge, Jinja — 525 m [7]
  • Industrial parks linked to regional corridors

6,850 kmpaved of a 21,000 km network [1]

03 · EDUCATION

A generation in school

Universal Primary Education opened the gates in 1997. Enrolment rose toward universal levels and adult literacy climbed from 43% to above 70%.

  • Universal Primary Education since 1997
  • Vocational and university enrolment expanding

43% → 70%+adult literacy [4]

04 · HEALTH

Care extended into every district

Regional referral capacity was rebuilt after conflict, neonatal and maternal care extended into rural clinics, and districts and cities grew from 33 to 146 as services followed people.

  • Neonatal and maternal care in rural clinics
  • National immunisation coverage expanded

33 → 146districts and cities [2]

05 · GROWTH & REFUGE

An economy rebuilt, a door held open

Steady growth returned: GDP per capita tripled and poverty fell from 56% to 16%. Under the Refugee Act of 2006, the country hosts 1.8 million people — among the most of any nation on earth.

  • GDP per capita $330 → $986 [4]
  • Poverty 56% → 16% [4]
  • Bidi Bidi among the world's largest settlements

1.8Mrefugees hosted [5]
